Juliet Lacey

Bryn Mawr College | Little City Books

I’m Juliet Lacey, a writer from New York. I recently moved to Los Angeles for the reasons most people move to LA (a love of film and the sun). My background spans from journalism, creative writing, and philosophy to administrative assistance and in-depth experience with strategic communications.

In college, I was an editor for Milkweed Magazine and a leader of the improv comedy group The Throng. I also created and co-hosted a workshop called Writing Across Identity, which you can learn more about here. Outside of school, I worked as a company representative and bookseller at Little City Books. Our social media accounts (Instagram and TikTok) also thrived. As a company representative, I attended events with MacMillan and Hachette publishing houses and edited manuscripts with Edelweiss.

In January of 2022, I began working at Student Sponsor Partners (SSP) in New York City as a Programs Associate. My role included cross-functional data management and analysis, detail-oriented scheduling, and pitching and writing the monthly newsletter. In developing strategic communications, I led a mentorship advice series as well as created and produced a video series for our admissions team. For both projects, we met our goal to create accessible information for a multi-faceted client base and increase our social engagement by 45x.

In my free time, I’m writing a play with my dear friend and comedy producer Leah Gayle Williams, performing improv, playing my guitar, and being a mentor to a wonderful high school student, Solenny.

Now that I’m based in LA, I am eager to begin my career on this coast. Feel free to reach out with any business opportunities!
